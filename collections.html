The Atlantic - Collections

A new way to create and exhibit series of stories

In collaboration with Caitlyn Hampton, I designed Collections, a story building tool.


The Problem

Current collections of stories (channel pages, categories, special projects) are stale, inflexible, and do little more than list out articles. Additionally, the distinctions and differences between each are not obvious or useful to readers.


Research

  • Stakeholder interviews to determine use cases of Collections tool
    • Interviewed edit, design, sales, data, and product teams
  • Competitive research of other landing pages that provide delightful and immersive experiences to readers
  • User stories: who (are our users), what (is the need to meet), wow (how to measure success)?
    • ex) A reader found a special project on Twitter, read at least 2 stories, and then tweeted the special project himself.

Goals

  • Empower editors with the flexibility to create story collection layouts that highlight their editorial coverage while impressing readers
  • Give readers collection pages that don't just host articles, but serve as rich story-telling pages themselves
  • Reduce # of unique page builds by the product team, saving time & resources

From this research phase, we set out to build an internal tool for Atlantic folks to externally output collections of stories, all created with editorial storytelling and reader delight in mind.
